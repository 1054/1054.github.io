DeepFields

Deep Fields are tiny windows to the galaxies in the early Universe. We learn how the Universe was more than thirteen billion years ago only through these windows.

In this project, we studied thousands of distant galaxies from redshift 0 to 6 in the GOODS-North field, in order to best measure their dust emission and estimate dust-obscured star formation rates hence the cosmic star formation rate density (D. Liu et al. 2018, ApJ).

Due to current limitations on the sensitivity and spatial resolution of  far-infrared and single-dish (sub-)millimeter observations, we can only study a few tens of dusty galaxies in the early Universe. This will be changed with the increasing number of surveys with (sub-)millimeter interferometry. Dusty galaxies and the dust content in more numerous star-forming galaxies in the early Universe will be understood in the near future.
