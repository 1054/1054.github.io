SpireLines

Page 1

We probe galaxies not only through images but also through spectral lines. Carbon monoxide and many other molecules and atoms help us to see the subtle physics even in most distant galaxies.

In this project, we studied hundreds of galaxies from local Universe to high redshift. All the local galaxies have Herschel Space Observatory spectral line observations, which covers about ten high rotational transition (high-J) CO lines, neutral atomic carbon and nitrogen lines and water vapour lines, etc.

We have independently reduced the full science archive for less than two hundred local galaxies observed with the Herschel SPIRE FTS instrument. We focused on the correlation between high-J CO lines and beam-matched infrared photometry, in order to understand the origin of these high-J CO lines and whether they have common properties. The answers are: mid-J CO lines are mostly powered by the star formation activity in galaxies, and yes, star formation has common properties. (D. Liu et al. 2015, ApJ).
