Radio - Radio Line Brightness Tempertature

Radio Line Brightness Tempertature Equations

$ L_{\mathrm{MoleLine}} = 4 \pi D_L^2 F_{\mathrm{obs}} = 4 \pi D_L^2 \ \nu_{\mathrm{obs}} \ c^{-1} ( \Sigma \Delta S_{\mathrm{obs}} v_{\mathrm{obs}} ) \quad [L_{\odot}] $

$ L\prime_{\mathrm{MoleLine}} = \mathrm{Area} * F_{\mathrm{source}} = \Omega_{s} D_A^2 (\Sigma T_{s} \Delta v_{s}) = D_L^2 \ \nu_{\mathrm{obs}}^{-2} \ \frac{c^2}{2k_B} (1+z)^{-3} ( \Sigma \Delta S_{\mathrm{obs}} v_{\mathrm{obs}} ) \quad [K \ km/s \ pc^2] $

where $ ( \Sigma \Delta S_{\mathrm{obs}} v_{\mathrm{obs}} ) $ has an unit of $ [\mathrm{Jy km/s}] $.

$ L\prime_{\mathrm{MoleLine}} = Area * F_{\mathrm{source}} = \Omega_{s} D_A^2 (\Sigma T_{s} \Delta v_{s}) = \Omega_{s*b} D_A^2 \ I_{\mathrm{obs}} \ (1+z) \quad [\mathrm{K} \ \mathrm{km}/\mathrm{s} \ \mathrm{pc}^2] $

where $ I_{\mathrm{obs}} $ has an unit of $ [\mathrm{K} \ \mathrm{km}/\mathrm{s}] $.

W m-2 to solar Lumin

Jy km s$^{-1}$ to solar Lumin

If flux $F$ has an unit $Jy \, km \, s^{-1}$, then

$ \frac{L}{\mathrm{[erg \ s^{-1}]}} = \frac{F}{\mathrm{[Jy \, km \, s^{-1}]}} \cdot \frac{\mathrm{[km/s]}}{c} \cdot \frac{\nu_{\mathrm{obs}}}{[\mathrm{Hz}]} \cdot 4 \pi \frac{D_\mathrm{L}^2}{[\mathrm{cm}]^2} $

$ \frac{L}{[erg\ s^{-1}]} = 3.9910769 \times 10^{30} \cdot \frac{F}{\mathrm{[Jy \, km \, s^{-1}]}} \cdot \frac{\nu_{\mathrm{obs}}}{[\mathrm{GHz}]} \cdot \frac{D_\mathrm{L}^2}{\mathrm{[Mpc]}^2} $

$ \frac{L}{[\mathrm{L}_{\odot}]} = 1.0339577 \times 10^{-3} \cdot \frac{F}{\mathrm{[Jy \, km \, s^{-1}]}} \cdot \frac{\nu_{\mathrm{obs}}}{\mathrm{[GHz]}} \cdot \frac{D_\mathrm{L}^2}{\mathrm{[Mpc]}^2} $

note that $\nu_{obs}$ is the observed frequency, rather than the rest frame frequency.

K km s$^{-1}$ to solar Lumin

$ \frac{L\prime_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \ km \ s^{-1} pc^2}]} = 3.25 \times 10^7 \ (\frac{D_\mathrm{L}}{[\mathrm{Mpc}]})^2 \ (\frac{\nu_{\mathrm{obs}}}{[\mathrm{GHz}]})^{-2} \ (\frac{S_{\mathrm{mol}} \Delta v}{[\mathrm{Jy \ km/s}]}) \ (1+z)^{-3} $

$ \frac{L}{[\mathrm{L}_{\odot}]} = 1.0339577 \times 10^{-3} (\frac{D_L}{[Mpc]})^2 (\frac{\nu_{obs}}{[\mathrm{GHz}]}) \frac{S_{\mathrm{mol}} \Delta v}{\mathrm{[Jy \, km \, s^{-1}]}}$

thus we have

$ \frac{L}{[\mathrm{L}_{\odot}]} = \frac{L\prime_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \ km \ s^{-1} pc^2}]} \times 3.1814084 \times 10^{-11} (\frac{\nu_{\mathrm{rest}}}{[\mathrm{GHz}]})^3 $

Jy km s$^{-1}$ to K km s$^{-1}$ pc$^2$

$ \frac{L\prime_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \ km \ s^{-1} pc^2}]} = 3.25 \times 10^7 \ (\frac{D_\mathrm{L}}{[\mathrm{Mpc}]})^2 \ (\frac{\nu_{\mathrm{obs}}}{[\mathrm{GHz}]})^{-2} \ (\frac{S_{\mathrm{mol}} \Delta v}{[\mathrm{Jy \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) \ (1+z)^{-3} $

see Solomon \& Vanden Bout 2005 ARA\&A eq (3).

or

$ \frac{L\prime_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \ km \ s^{-1} pc^2}]} = 3.25 \times 10^7 \ (\frac{D_\mathrm{L}}{[\mathrm{Mpc}]})^2 \ (\frac{\nu_{\mathrm{rest}}}{[\mathrm{GHz}]})^{-2} \ (\frac{S_{\mathrm{mol}} \Delta v}{[\mathrm{Jy \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) \ (1+z)^{-1} $

K km s$^{-1}$ to K km s$^{-1}$ pc$^2$

$ \frac{L\prime_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \ km \ s^{-1} pc^2}]} = \mathrm{Area} \cdot (\frac{I_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) $

$ = (\frac{\Omega_{s*b}}{[\mathrm{sr}]}) \ (\frac{D_\mathrm{A}}{[\mathrm{pc}]})^2 \ (\frac{I_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) $

$ = (\frac{\Omega_{s*b}}{[\mathrm{arcsec}^2]}) \ (\frac{\pi\times10^{6}}{180\times3600})^2 \ (\frac{D_\mathrm{L}}{[\mathrm{Mpc}]})^2 \ (\frac{I_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) \ (1+z)^{-3} $

thus

$ \frac{L\prime_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \ km \ s^{-1} pc^2}]} = 23.50443 \ (\frac{\Omega_{s*b}}{[\mathrm{arcsec}^2]}) \ (\frac{D_\mathrm{L}}{[\mathrm{Mpc}]})^2 \ (\frac{I_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) \ (1+z)^{-3} $

see Solomon \& Vanden Bout 2005 ARA\&A eq (2).

and consider Gaussian shape beam, $\Omega_{s*b} = \frac{\pi}{4 \ln 2} \theta_{\mathrm{FHWM}}^2$

thus

$ \frac{L\prime_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \ km \ s^{-1} pc^2}]} = 26.63263542 \ (\frac{\theta_{\mathrm{FHWM}}^2}{[\mathrm{arcsec}^2]}) \ (\frac{D_\mathrm{L}}{[\mathrm{Mpc}]})^2 \ (\frac{I_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) \ (1+z)^{-3} $

see Gao \& Solomon 2004 ApJS eq (2).

$ 3.25 \times 10^7 \ (\frac{\nu_{\mathrm{rest}}}{[\mathrm{GHz}]})^{-2} \ (\frac{S_{\mathrm{mol}} \Delta v}{[\mathrm{Jy \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) = 26.63263542 \ (\frac{\theta_{\mathrm{FHWM}}^2}{[\mathrm{arcsec}^2]}) \ (\frac{I_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) \ (1+z)^{-2} $

$\to$ $ (\frac{S_{\mathrm{mol}} \Delta v}{[\mathrm{Jy \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) = 8.195 \times 10^{-7} \ (\frac{\theta_{\mathrm{FHWM}}^2}{[\mathrm{arcsec}^2]}) \ (\frac{\nu_{\mathrm{rest}}}{[\mathrm{GHz}]})^{2} \ (\frac{I_{\mathrm{mol}}}{[\mathrm{K \, km \, s^{-1}}]}) \ (1+z)^{-2} $

Radiative Transfer Calculation Equations

Calculating [CI]

$T_{\mathrm{R}} = T_{0} \left(\frac{1}{e^{T_{0}/T_{ex}}-1} - \frac{1}{e^{T_{0}/T_{bg}}-1} \right) (1-e^{-\tau})$, where $T_{0} \equiv h \nu / k$.

Applying $\tau \gg 1$, $T_{bg} = 2.73 \ \mathrm{K}$, $T_{0,\mathrm{CO(1-0)}} = 5.5 \ \mathrm{K}$, we have:

$T_{R,\mathrm{CO(1-0)},max} = 5.5/{(e^{5.5/T_{ex}}-1)} - 0.846$

So:

$T_{ex} = 5.5 \, / \left( {\ln [ 1 + 5.5/(T_{R,\mathrm{CO(1-0)},max} + 0.846) ]} \right)$

Then we can calculate the optical depth $\tau$ for [CI] with:

$T_{R,\mathrm{[CI](1-0)}} = T_{0,\mathrm{[CI](1-0)}} \left( \frac{1}{e^{T_{0,\mathrm{[CI](1-0)}}/T_{ex}}-1} - \frac{1}{e^{T_{0,\mathrm{[CI](1-0)}}/T_{bg}}-1} \right) (1 - e^{-\tau})$

Applying $T_{bg} = 2.73 \ \mathrm{K}$, $T_{0,\mathrm{[CI](1-0)}} = 24 \ \mathrm{K}$, we have:

$(1 - e^{-\tau}) = \frac{T_{R,\mathrm{[CI](1-0)}}}{T_{0,\mathrm{[CI](1-0)}}} \left( \frac{1}{e^{24/T_{ex}}-1} - \frac{1}{e^{24/2.73}-1} \right)^{-1} $
