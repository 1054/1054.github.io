OPD = Optical Path Difference

The SPIRE Fourier-Transform Spectrometer (FTS) uses the principle of interferometry. FTS split the input photons into two arrays then correlate them with a range of OPD and record the photon-induced electron signal (interferogram signal, in units of Voigt) at each OPD. An inverse Fourier transform to the interferogram signal as a function of 1/OPD (a.k.a. wavenumber, in units of $\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$) will produce the physical flux density spectrum (in units of Jansky) as a function of frequency (in units of Hz, $\mathrm{frequency} = c / \mathrm{wavelength}$, where $c$ is the speed of light).
